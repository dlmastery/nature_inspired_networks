\section{Introduction}
% =====================================================================
\label{sec:intro}

Geometric Deep Learning (GDL) and Topological Deep Learning (TDL) have
repeatedly converged on a small set of geometric priors --- hexagonal
lattices~\citep{hoogeboom2018hexaconv}, Platonic / icosahedral symmetry
groups~\citep{cohen2019spherical, islam2025platonic}, fractal
recursion~\citep{larsson2017fractalnet}, toroidal
embeddings~\citep{pittorino2022toroidal}, Fibonacci channels, and
Hopfield / SIREN / RoPE phase
encodings~\citep{ramsauer2021hopfield, sitzmann2020siren, su2024rope}.
\citet{bronstein2021gdl} systematise much of this surface. A separate
popular literature suggests the golden ratio $\varphi \approx 1.618$
and Fibonacci sequence ``appear everywhere in nature,'' and it is
tempting to assemble the geometric priors and the $\varphi$-flavoured
constants into one framework and claim broad gains.

\textbf{This work does not do that.} It presents the \emph{infrastructure
required to know whether such a claim is justified}, and applies that
infrastructure to its own design space. We implement 84 hypotheses
faithfully, audit them adversarially with two independent expert teams,
patch the findings, re-run the empirical evidence on the corrected code,
and publish the surviving framework with its honest verdict --- including
the honest verdict that the protocol's own empirical findings are
\emph{provisional} until the Phase-9 hill-climb completes.

\subsection{Contributions}
\label{sec:contributions}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{A dual-track skeptical audit protocol.} An 8-agent
    implementation-critic team verifies \emph{code-vs-doc correspondence};
    an 8-agent research-scientist-critic team challenges the
    \emph{scientific merit} of each hypothesis. Both teams run in
    parallel with disjoint file scopes.
  \item \textbf{A Fixer campaign with mechanism-verifying-test
    discipline.} Every code patch ships with at least one test that
    asserts the fixed mechanism --- the test the original implementer
    missed. Fixers correct 22 hypotheses across 8 commits, adding
    $\sim$34 new mechanism tests.
  \item \textbf{A normative ruleset} (\texttt{CLAUDE.md} Rules~20--28)
    operationalising the audit + fix + re-run cycle and the
    screening-vs-evaluation distinction, plus seven reusable
    content-agnostic skills.
  \item \textbf{A reproducible 84-hypothesis implementation} of
    nature-inspired neural priors as 80 pure-PyTorch modules + 78 test
    files ($\sim$826 unit tests post-Fixer), each with a
    committee-grade design doc.
  \item \textbf{Empirical calibration on CIFAR-10/-100}: 51\% non-PASS
    implementation-critic rate (3 BROKEN / 15 MAJOR / 24 MINOR of 83);
    1\,/\,81 NOVEL+TESTABLE sci-critic rate; three Phase-8 candidates
    pass the worst-leader-seed gate.
\end{enumerate}

What is \emph{explicitly not} a contribution: any claim about
transformer-track hypotheses (10 of 84 target attention backbones, none
tested); any claim about H71 IcosaRoPE3D (the sole NOVEL+TESTABLE
survivor is untested); any claim about cross-domain portability (open
future work); any claim that the three Phase-8 winners constitute a
standalone empirical headline --- they are illustrative protocol
output.

\subsection{Why the audit was necessary}
\label{sec:why-audit}

The pre-audit version of this paper claimed H09 \texttt{phi\_budget}
as a verified cross-dataset positive (CIFAR-10 85.54\%, CIFAR-100
58.05\% 3-seed median; $+1.53$\,pp over baseline). Every test in the
repository had been written by an implementer agent trained to make
tests pass, and ``make tests pass'' often degenerates to shape-only
assertions. The audit revealed that H09's realised stage-parameter
ratio was $1{:}1.41{:}2.45$, not the claimed
$1{:}\varphi{:}\varphi^2 = 1{:}1.618{:}2.618$ (12.6\% drift at
stage~1). The headline was produced by a network that did \emph{not}
faithfully implement its own design doc. Fixer-PhiScaling (commit
\texttt{519cdf3}) corrected the integer search; the post-fix realised
ratio is $1{:}1.623{:}2.629$ (0.43\% max error). This is the
protocol's load-bearing example of diagnostic power.

\subsection{Limitations of the audit protocol --- auditor self-grading}
\label{sec:self-grading}

A binding caveat: implementer, implementation-critic, sci-critic,
Fixer, and audit-calibration agents in this campaign are \emph{all}
from the same model family (Claude Opus 4.7). Disjoint-scope
independence is enforced; model-family independence is not. The
audit's 51\% non-PASS rate is conditional on this caveat. Third-party
code calibration (\S\ref{sec:third-party}) partially neutralises it; a
cross-family audit on the same \texttt{pytorch/vision} sample remains
future work.

\section{The Dual-Track Audit + Fixer Protocol}
% =====================================================================
\label{sec:protocol}

The autoresearch protocol of \texttt{dlmastery/autoresearchimage} ---
citation rigor (every reasoning entry uses
\texttt{Author YEAR VENUE 'Title' (arXiv:XXXX.XXXXX)}), reasoning-blob
completeness (word-count floors), SHA-256-fingerprinted composite
metric, append-only experiment log, no-bypass gates, per-experiment
archive directory --- is taken verbatim and forms the \emph{floor}.
Rules~20--28 layered on top during this campaign codify the
auto-checkpoint loop, post-fix re-run discipline, dual-track audit
gate, orthogonal-axes-only compounding, dashboard discipline,
Q\&A-test correspondence, Windows thread-cap safety, Pages-link
discipline, and screening-vs-evaluation tiering.

\subsection{Track A: implementation-critic team}
\label{sec:track-a}

For each thematic group G1--G8, a parallel Critic agent reads every
hypothesis's design doc, the corresponding \texttt{src} module, and
the test file. Findings on six dimensions: (1)~mechanism check;
(2)~math correctness; (3)~test rigor --- does the test assert
\emph{mechanism} or only \emph{shape}; (4)~citation alignment;
(5)~falsifier reachability; (6)~hidden bugs / cargo-cult. Verdict
tiers: PASS / MINOR (shape-only tests; cosmetic) / MAJOR (mechanism
partially wrong) / BROKEN (code contradicts the doc).

\subsection{Track B: research-scientist-critic team}
\label{sec:track-b}

Independent of the implementation, each group's sci-critic appends an
``Addendum: Research-Scientist Critique'' to every design doc,
challenging prior plausibility, mechanism scrutiny, confounds
($\geq 2$), \emph{numerology check} (does $\varphi$ specifically
matter, or would any value in $[1.3, 2.0]$ work?), literature
precedent, expected effect size with 90\% CI, and a
minimum-distinguishing experiment. Verdict: NOVEL+TESTABLE /
DERIVATIVE+TESTABLE / NUMEROLOGY / FALSIFIED / UNFALSIFIABLE /
INFRASTRUCTURE.

\subsection{Track C: Fixer campaign}
\label{sec:fixer}

Findings from Track A populate the Fixer specs. Eight parallel Fixer
agents (partitioned by primary \texttt{src} file to avoid
cross-fixer contention) (a)~patch the code per the audit's ``Concrete
fix'' instruction, (b)~add at least one
\emph{mechanism-verifying} test that would have caught the bug
(\emph{not} shape-only), (c)~confirm the test passes, (d)~commit with
retry-wrapped scoped \texttt{git add}.

Together: 8 commits, $\sim$34 new mechanism tests, $\sim$16 patched
\texttt{src} files. After all Fixers land, every affected sweep row
re-runs on the corrected code (Rule~21). The mechanism-pinning test
is the durable artefact --- the patched code may improve further, but
the test invariant remains.

The canonical example is H09 \texttt{phi\_budget}. The Fixer replaced
a greedy integer search whose drift produced $1{:}1.41{:}2.45$ with a
constrained search that minimises max-error against
$\{1, \varphi, \varphi^2\}$:

\begin{lstlisting}[language=Python, caption={Fixer-PhiScaling mechanism-pinning test (\texttt{tests/test\_phi\_budget.py}, abbreviated). The test would have caught the original 12.6\,\% drift.}, label={lst:phi-test}]
def test_phi_budget_realised_ratio():
    """Realised stage-parameter ratio
    must match 1:phi:phi^2 within 1%."""
    widths = phi_budget_widths(
        target_params=270_000,
        n_stages=3, phi=1.618033988749)
    ratio = [w / widths[0] for w in widths]
    expected = [1.0, 1.618, 2.618]
    max_err = max(abs(r - e) / e
                  for r, e in zip(
                      ratio, expected))
    assert max_err < 0.01, (
      f"realised ratio {ratio} drifts "
      f"{max_err*100:.2f}% from "
      f"1:phi:phi^2")
\end{lstlisting}

\subsection{Operational guarantees}
\label{sec:rules-summary}

Rules~20--28 give the protocol its empirical contract:
\textbf{Rule~21} (\emph{post-fix re-run discipline}) forbids any
external claim drawn from pre-fix code; \textbf{Rule~22} (\emph{dual-track
audit gate}) requires both PASS impl-critic and a non-NUMEROLOGY
sci-critic before a hypothesis becomes a headline; \textbf{Rule~23}
(\emph{orthogonal axes only}) forbids stacking $> 2$ priors on the same
conv-block path; \textbf{Rule~28} (\emph{screening vs evaluation})
labels all single-seed sweep data as screening, with evaluation
reserved for $\geq 3$-seed multi-seed gates.

\section{Methods}
% =====================================================================
\label{sec:methods}

\subsection{Composite metric and SHA-256 fingerprint}
\label{sec:composite}

The composite metric (Rule~2) is
\begin{equation}
\textsf{composite} = \mathrm{top1} - 0.05 \log_{10}(\mathrm{params}_M)
                                  - 0.05 \log_{10}(\mathrm{latency}_{\mathrm{ms}}).
\label{eq:composite}
\end{equation}
The source string of Eq.~\ref{eq:composite} is SHA-256-fingerprinted
to \texttt{d65565e9c7b12d14cbce30a801ecc6753aea3eb148074256bfcc051fa61d0893};
editing it raises a \texttt{CompositeFingerprintError} at runner
import time. A new branch is required to change it.

\subsection{Screening sweep design}
\label{sec:screening}

\textbf{Hardware (Rule~26).} 1$\times$~RTX~4090 Laptop, 16~GB VRAM,
Windows~11; Python~3.13; bf16 AMP; \texttt{num\_workers=0};
\texttt{KMP\_DUPLICATE\_LIB\_OK=TRUE},
\texttt{OMP\_NUM\_THREADS=2}, \texttt{MKL\_NUM\_THREADS=2};
\texttt{set\_seed(seed)} at run start; \texttt{cudnn.benchmark=True}
(not bit-reproducible by design; Rule~6).

\textbf{Training defaults} (CIFAR-10 12-ep screening; CIFAR-100 30-ep
graduation + Phase-8 $n{=}7$). AdamW~\citep{loshchilov2019adamw};
LR~$10^{-3}$ (cosine, $T_{\max}{=}$epochs); weight decay $5{\times}10^{-4}$;
batch~256; label smoothing 0.1; RandomCrop(32, pad=4) + HorizontalFlip
+ RandAugment~\citep{cubuk2020randaugment} ($N{=}1$, $M{=}4$); bf16.
Single seed for 32/35 CIFAR-10 screening rows; $n{=}7$ for the three
Phase-8 winners on CIFAR-100.

\subsection{Statistical-rigor floor}
\label{sec:stats-floor}

Phase-8 candidates clear a three-layer gate: (i)~paired Wilcoxon at
$\alpha{=}0.05$ on the $n{=}7$ CIFAR-100 deltas; (ii)~Holm--Bonferroni
step-down at $\alpha'{=}0.05/k$ for $k{=}3$ confirmatory
tests~\citep{holm1979}; (iii)~Phase-5 ordinal gate
$\min(\text{leader}) > \max(\text{baseline})$. At $n{=}7$ with 7/7
positive deltas the Wilcoxon $p$ achieves its theoretical floor
$(1/2)^7 = 0.0078$, which clears the Holm step-down at $\alpha/3 =
0.0167$. Magnitude is reported by a paired $t$-test (df=6) whose
$p$-values fall 3--4 orders of magnitude below the Wilcoxon floor.

NOVEL+TESTABLE: 1 (H71 IcosaRoPE3D, untested); DERIVATIVE+TESTABLE:

\subsection{BROKEN findings (case studies of protocol output)}
\label{sec:broken}

\paragraph{H55 PlatonicAttention's head bias is mathematically zero.}
\texttt{bias = (coords @ coords.T).mean(dim=-1)} evaluates to all
zeros for every vertex-transitive Platonic solid (vertex coords sum
to the centroid). PlatonicAttention was bit-equivalent to vanilla
multi-head attention. All 7 of its tests were shape-only. \emph{Fixed}
in Fixer-G6 (\texttt{16fe2b6}) with a relative-position cosine bias
mirroring the H37 pentagonal pattern~\citep{islam2025platonic}.

\paragraph{H67 hybrid\_full was a half-on stress test.}
\texttt{from .golden\_rope import GoldenRoPE} raised
\texttt{ImportError}; \texttt{MetatronGraphLayer}'s constructor
signature was wrong; \texttt{which\_priors\_active} hardcoded
\texttt{True} for 4 priors; \texttt{LiquidCFC} collapsed to affine +
nonlinearity. \emph{Fixed} in Fixer-G7 (\texttt{2e7ee45}).

\paragraph{H74 MetatronTiedConv2d's 13 alphas collapsed to one scalar.}
Forward was \texttt{F.conv2d(x, W * sum(alpha\_c))}; the 13 alphas
$\Sigma$-summed to a single gate. \emph{Fixed} in Fixer-G7 to use
H40's \texttt{metatron\_basis\_kernels} 13 spatially-distinct circle
masks; \texttt{effective\_weight = sum\_c alpha\_c * (W * mask\_c)}.

\subsection{The H09 phi\_budget case study}
\label{sec:h09}

\S\ref{sec:why-audit} describes the realised-ratio drift
($1{:}1.41{:}2.45$ vs.\ claimed $1{:}\varphi{:}\varphi^2$) and
Fixer-PhiScaling's correction. Post-fix 3-seed CIFAR-100 medians on
the corrected mechanism: \texttt{phi\_budget}=0.5741,
\texttt{baseline}=0.5652 ($+0.89$\,pp). The pre-fix median (58.05\%)
was $\sim 0.6$\,pp \emph{higher} than the post-fix (57.41\%),
consistent with ``the broken realised ratio happened to land a
fortuitously-high seed-0 result.'' The protocol caught a code-vs-doc
divergence that would have shipped as a headline in a non-audited
pipeline; whether the post-fix $+0.89$\,pp represents a genuine
architectural signal or a tuning artifact is decided by Phase-9
hill-climb, not this submission.

Table~\ref{tab:phase-8} summarises the n=7 certification.

\textbf{Magnitude diagnostic.} The Wilcoxon-at-floor at $n{=}7$ is
informationally equivalent to a paired sign test. The paired
$t$-test (df=6) extracts $\sigma$-scaled magnitude information and
produces $p$-values 3--4 orders of magnitude below the floor:
$t=9.06, p=5{\times}10^{-5}$ for \texttt{pair\_gm\_pdw};
$t=7.82, p=1.2{\times}10^{-4}$ for \texttt{slot\_act\_sine};
$t=5.43, p=8.1{\times}10^{-4}$ for \texttt{sg\_only\_phi\_budget}.

\textbf{Hill-climbed robustness} (Phase-9a, $n{=}3$). A per-hypothesis
coordinate hill-climb (cube lr$\times$wd$\times$bs$\times$optimizer,
budget 25) ran independently on baseline and each leader.
Hill-climbed-baseline median = 0.5929 at \{lr=$3{\times}10^{-3}$,
wd=$5{\times}10^{-4}$, bs=256, AdamW\}. Hill-climbed leader $\Delta$
means: \texttt{slot\_act\_sine} $+1.31$\,pp (CI $[+0.20, +2.23]$),
\texttt{pair\_gm\_pdw} $+1.22$\,pp (CI $[+0.15, +1.99]$),
\texttt{sg\_only\_phi\_budget} $+0.79$\,pp (CI
$\mathbf{[-0.32, +1.76]}$ --- \emph{includes 0}). We report this
honestly: the n=7 default-config certification stands, but the
hill-climbed CI for \texttt{sg\_only\_phi\_budget} undercuts it when
the analysis is tuned-vs-tuned. The two-arm batch-size confound (all
three winners' \texttt{best\_config} is bs=128 vs baseline bs=256)
is filed as Phase-9d.

\textbf{Iso-tuned-cell extension} (Phase-9a baseline-extension,
$n{=}3$). At the iso-tuned cell (lr=$3{\times}10^{-3}$,
wd=$5{\times}10^{-4}$, bs=128, AdamW), $\sigma_{\mathrm{iso}}=1.14$\,pp
--- 2.5$\times$ wider than $\sigma_{\mathrm{default}}=0.453$\,pp. All
three $\Delta$ means fall \emph{inside} the iso-tuned
$2\sigma_{\mathrm{iso}}=2.28$\,pp band but \emph{outside} the default
$2\sigma_{\mathrm{default}}=0.91$\,pp band. The Phase-5 ordinal gate
fails for all three at iso-tuned $n{=}3$. Honest framing: the
$n{=}7$ default-config certification stands because
$\sigma_{\mathrm{default}}$ is small at $n{=}7$; the iso-tuned
extension confirms directional positive $\Delta$ but cannot itself
re-certify at NeurIPS $\alpha$. Phase-9f ($n{=}7$+ iso-tuned
baseline-and-leader extension; $\sim 14$ GPU-h) would deliver
Wilcoxon floor 0.0078 in the iso-tuned regime and is filed as future
work.

\textbf{POSI family-size re-framing.} The $k{=}3$ Holm--Bonferroni
family is the \emph{confirmatory} family, not the screening family.
Candidates were filtered from a $\sim$49-row decision population
(35-row CIFAR-10 screen + 7-row CIFAR-100 graduation + 7-row combo
ladder) by the Phase-5 ordinal gate at $n{=}3$, a pre-specified
criterion (Rule~28). POSI Holm--Bonferroni across the full 49-row
decision family would demand $\alpha' = 0.05/49 \approx 0.001$, which
the Wilcoxon $n{=}7$ floor of 0.0078 does not clear. The paired-$t$
magnitude $p$-values do clear that bound for \texttt{pair\_gm\_pdw}
and \texttt{slot\_act\_sine} but not for
\texttt{sg\_only\_phi\_budget}. Both bounds are reported.

\textbf{Honest reading.} \texttt{pair\_gm\_pdw} stacks three
orthogonal axes; its $\Delta$mean $+1.74$\,pp is certified at the
family-of-3 bound but does \emph{not} resolve the non-$\varphi$
3-axis regularizer confound (Controls~1--4 in
\texttt{controls/PLAN.md} are READY but unlaunched).
\texttt{slot\_act\_sine} swaps ReLU for $\sin(\omega x)$ with
$\omega{=}1$; the lift is consistent with~\citet{sitzmann2020siren}
SIREN's known benefit and has \emph{no $\varphi$ content}.
\texttt{sg\_only\_phi\_budget}'s sci-critic verdict remains
DERIVATIVE+TESTABLE --- a rediscovery of RegNet's Pareto region
($w_m \in [2.5, 2.9]$, not $\varphi=1.618$;
\citealp{radosavovic2020regnet}).

33.3\%). Project non-PASS: 50.6\%. \textbf{MINOR tier 29\% project
vs.\ 33\% calibration} --- comparable. \textbf{MAJOR/BROKEN tier 22\%
project vs.\ 0\% calibration} --- the diagnostically-credible
22\,pp excess. Formal interval analysis (100\,000-iter binomial
bootstrap, $\mathrm{rng}=20260530$): bootstrap 95\% CI on the
difference $[+13.3, +31.3]$\,pp (excludes 0); Wilson 95\% CIs project
$[14.2\%, 31.7\%]$, calibration $[0.0\%, 20.4\%]$; \textbf{Fisher
exact one-sided $p=0.036$} (clears $\alpha{=}0.05$), two-sided
$p=0.066$; pooled $z$-test two-sided $p=0.046$.

The 22-pp excess is \emph{directionally credible and one-sided
significant at $\alpha=0.05$} but does not clear two-sided
$\alpha{=}0.05$ under the conservative Fisher exact; $n\geq 50$
(Phase-9b --- timm + HF Transformers + Lightning Bolts) is required to
unambiguously certify. The MAJOR/BROKEN tier --- where H09
realised-ratio drift, H21 \texttt{hex\_phi} undocumented divergence,
and H67 broken \texttt{GoldenRoPE} import live --- is the protocol's
load-bearing diagnostic surface.

\textbf{Cross-family re-audit on 10 MAJOR/BROKEN verdicts (partial).}
A methodologically-diverse intra-family re-audit (three methods:
property-based, mechanism-trace, paper-math) across a stratified
10-finding subsample (including all 3 originally BROKEN) yields
\textbf{8/10 strict CONCORDANT, 10/10 defect-existence CONCORDANT}.
Honest limitation: this is not a non-Claude external auditor; the
GPT-5 / Gemini~3 Pro pass on the same subsample is filed as Phase-9e.

\section{Limitations and Threats to Validity}
% =====================================================================
\label{sec:limitations}

\textbf{Single-seed for most single-prior sweep rows.} Only
\texttt{baseline\_resnet20}, \texttt{phi\_budget}, and
\texttt{golden\_momentum} carry 3-seed on CIFAR-10 to date; the rest
are seed-0. Every per-tag empirical delta from the screening sweep is
single-seed and is treated as screening data per Rule~28.

\textbf{No transformer experiments.} Half the 84 hypotheses target
attention / sequence backbones; none are tested. ViT-Tiny + decoder-LM
scaffolds are filed as future work.

\textbf{No tuned-baseline or RegNet comparison.} The H09
$+0.78$\,pp screening lift could be a tuning artifact. Phase-9c filed.

\textbf{Auditor self-grading at the model-family level.} See
\S\ref{sec:self-grading}; the cross-family audit on the calibration
sample is open future work.

\textbf{POSI on Phase-8 family selection.} The $k{=}3$ confirmatory
family is post-screening (\S\ref{sec:phase-8}). We report both the
$k{=}3$ family-of-3 certification \emph{and} the $k{=}49$ POSI bound.
The paired-$t$ magnitude test clears 0.001 for two winners;
\texttt{sg\_only\_phi\_budget} does not.

\textbf{Calibration $n{=}15$.} Two-sided Fisher exact on the 22-pp
MAJOR/BROKEN excess is $p=0.066$ (one-sided $p=0.036$). Phase-9b
extends to $n\geq 50$.

\textbf{Hill-climbed \texttt{sg\_only\_phi\_budget} CI includes 0}
(\S\ref{sec:phase-8}). Reported honestly; the $n{=}7$ default-config
certification is the formal claim; the hill-climb is an additive
robustness extension.

\textbf{Iso-tuned-cell extension at $n{=}3$ has wide baseline
$\sigma$.} $\sigma_{\mathrm{iso}}{=}1.14$\,pp at $n{=}3$ vs
$\sigma_{\mathrm{default}}{=}0.45$\,pp at $n{=}7$. Iso-tuned
$\Delta$s of $+1.16$ to $+1.68$\,pp fall inside
$2\sigma_{\mathrm{iso}}$. The directional signal is preserved across
all three winners but cannot be formally certified at NeurIPS
$\alpha$ at this $n$. Phase-9f ($n{=}7+$ iso-tuned extension) is the
resolution path.

\textbf{CIFAR-10/-100 is not a sufficient testbed} for several
equivariance / topological hypotheses (rotated CIFAR, Spherical MNIST,
ModelNet40, ogbg-molhiv are appropriate but unbuilt).

\textbf{Future-work GPU pipeline.} Phase-9f / Wave-1 / Controls~1--4
in \texttt{controls/PLAN.md} are READY but unlaunched at submission
($\sim$31.75 GPU-h). Their closure is the principled path to a
non-provisional empirical headline.

\section{Related Work}
% =====================================================================
\label{sec:related}

\textbf{Geometric / topological priors.} The design space sits inside
the GDL taxonomy of~\citet{bronstein2021gdl}; specific anchors are
\citet{hoogeboom2018hexaconv} for hexagonal lattices,
\citet{cohen2019spherical} for spherical equivariance,
\citet{larsson2017fractalnet} for fractal recursion,
\citet{pittorino2022toroidal} for toroidal flat-minima geometry, and
\citet{islam2025platonic} for Platonic-solid attention biases.

\textbf{Width-progression / scaling.} \citet{radosavovic2020regnet}
established the Pareto region $w_m \in [2.5, 2.9]$ that
\emph{contains but does not require} $\varphi$; this is the literature
anchor for the H09 DERIVATIVE+TESTABLE verdict.
\citet{he2016resnet, huang2017densenet, liu2022convnext, bello2021resnetrs,
wightman2021rsb, hetricks2019} establish the convergence-budget
CIFAR-10 SOTA (\textgreater 91\%) that the screening sweep does not
attempt to reach.

\textbf{Optimisation and the H41 falsification.}
\citet{loshchilov2019adamw} provides the baseline AdamW;
\citet{reddi2018adam, choi2019optimizer, wilson2017marginal,
hoffer2017longer} ground the H41 long-horizon $\beta_2$ caveat.

\textbf{Activations and SIREN.} \citet{sitzmann2020siren} is the
literature anchor for \texttt{slot\_act\_sine}; this is also why the
\texttt{slot\_act\_sine} lift carries no $\varphi$ content.

\textbf{Augmentation and the regularizer band.}
\citet{cubuk2020randaugment, zhang2018mixup} bracket the augmentation
effect sizes; \citet{foret2021sam} gives the SAM regularizer upper
bound used in the magnitude-comparison band of \S\ref{sec:phase-8}.

\textbf{Multiple-comparisons.} \citet{holm1979} is the canonical
step-down Bonferroni used throughout \S\ref{sec:phase-8} and
\S\ref{sec:third-party}.

To our knowledge, no prior work systematically audits a
nature-inspired prior design space with two independent expert teams
and a mechanism-pinning Fixer campaign; the contribution is the
combination, not any individual prior.

\section{Conclusion}
% =====================================================================
\label{sec:conclusion}

\textbf{The protocol is the contribution.} After 84 hypothesis
implementations, 41+24+15+3 implementation verdicts, 1+30+40+3+2+5
scientific verdicts, 8 mechanism-correcting Fixer commits, and
$\sim$7 GPU-hours of CIFAR-10/-100 ablation, the protocol produced
calibration data: 51\% non-PASS impl-critic rate (with the 22-pp
MAJOR/BROKEN sub-tier excess as the diagnostically-credible signal,
one-sided Fisher $p=0.036$), 1\,/\,81 NOVEL+TESTABLE sci-critic rate,
and three protocol-screened candidates that pass a 7-seed CIFAR-100
30-ep paired Wilcoxon at $\alpha{=}0.05$ under Holm--Bonferroni across
the $k{=}3$ confirmatory family.

We make \emph{no} standalone empirical headline claim for
nature-inspired priors at this scale. The three Phase-8 candidates
are illustrative protocol output, conditional on Phase-9 hill-climb
and on the non-$\varphi$ control row (Controls~1--4, READY but
unlaunched). The single NOVEL+TESTABLE sci-critic survivor (H71
IcosaRoPE3D) is untested and is treated as a research proposal.

The audit + Fixer + re-run cycle, encoded in \texttt{CLAUDE.md}
Rules~20--28 and packaged as seven content-agnostic skills, is what
we offer the community: portable infrastructure for distinguishing
signal from numerology when an autoresearch design space is
irresistibly large and the experimental cost per hypothesis is
non-trivial. The cross-domain demonstration of that portability is
open future work; subject to those caveats, the protocol caught a
headline claim produced by broken code \emph{before} that claim went
to publication.\footnote{ICML 2027 reviewer reports are tracked in
\texttt{audits/ICML\_REVIEWS\_2026-05-30/}; the area-chair synthesis
and rebuttal are in
\texttt{audits/ICML\_REVIEWS\_2026-05-30/REBUTTAL.md}.}
