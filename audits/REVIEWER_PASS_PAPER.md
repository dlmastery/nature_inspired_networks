# NeurIPS-grade reviewer pass — PAPER.md
Reviewer: hostile area-chair, 2026-05-29.
Verdict: **WEAK_REJECT** (revise-and-resubmit; the methodological contribution is salvageable but the paper as written commits multiple BLOCKER-level rigor failures that no top-tier venue should accept).
Overall summary: The paper's "the contribution is the protocol" framing is genuinely interesting and the dual-track audit + Fixer machinery is novel infrastructure. However, (1) the headline empirical claims (`pair_gm_pdw`, `slot_act_sine`, post-fix `phi_budget`) are decorated with statistical language ("outside seed noise") that the actual data — n=3 seeds, no formal test, no confidence intervals, no Holm-Bonferroni across multi-row screening — cannot support; (2) the paper repeatedly hedges its way out of falsifications (§7.3.1 reclassifies a 35-row sweep as "screening, not evaluation" AFTER the negatives surfaced, a textbook post-hoc protocol amendment); and (3) the sci-critic gate has been gerrymandered: H09 is `DERIVATIVE` (the paper's own §5.4 says it is a RegNet rediscovery — Radosavovic 2020 *explicitly* prefers `w_m ∈ [2.5, 2.9]`, NOT φ=1.618; see `audits/PAPER_GAP_G1.md` H04), yet the abstract still markets it as a contribution. Until §B/C/D below are addressed the paper is publishable only as a methods note on the protocol with the empirical claims removed.

## A. Novelty / Contribution

- **PAPER.md:9 (Abstract)** — The abstract presents both "the protocol is the contribution" AND a positive empirical headline ("strongest provisional positive ... beats the ResNet-20 baseline ... but only the post-fix re-run will confirm"). This is **internally contradictory** — the abstract should pick ONE story. As written it sells the priors and the protocol simultaneously. **MAJOR.**
- **PAPER.md:3 (Final verdict banner)** — Claims `pair_gm_pdw` is "the project's first experimental evidence of orthogonal-axis prior compounding." This is **a novelty overclaim**: orthogonal-axis compounding is the entire literature on regularization stacking (Dropout + WD + LR schedule + augmentation — see Loshchilov 2019 AdamW, He 2019 "Bag of Tricks", Hoffer 2018 "Train longer"). Three orthogonal regularizers helping additively is not a discovery; it is the null hypothesis. **BLOCKER.**
- **PAPER.md:17–23 (Contributions 1–5)** — Contribution 5 ("an honest empirical verdict") is not a contribution; it is a result. Contribution 4 (Rules 20–25 + 7 skills) is project-internal book-keeping with no demonstrated transferability to a second autoresearch domain. The paper claims content-agnostic portability (line 22) but provides zero evidence (e.g., a cross-domain replication on tabular/medical). **MAJOR.**
- **PAPER.md:111 (H71 NOVEL+TESTABLE)** — H71 IcosaRoPE3D is listed as the SOLE NOVEL+TESTABLE survivor and is **never empirically tested**. Counting it as a contribution while §7.4 admits "no CIFAR smoke yet" is rhetorical contraband: an untested novel idea is a research proposal, not a result. **MAJOR.**
- **PAPER.md:13–14 (Introduction)** — Frames the paper as "the opposite of [claiming broad gains]" — but §5.5 (lines 132–134) then claims `pair_gm_pdw` as a NEW HEADLINE. The introduction's hedged stance is undermined by the body's marketing. **MINOR.**
- **PAPER.md:9, 229 (sole NOVEL+TESTABLE claim)** — The 1/81 NOVEL+TESTABLE rate is presented as a finding, but the sci-critic verdict tiers themselves were authored by *the same project* that proposed the hypotheses. Self-grading with disjoint subteams is not the same as external peer review, and the paper does not engage this circularity. **MAJOR.**
- **PAPER.md:179 (H09 phi_budget)** — Lists H09 as eligible for external claims despite the paper itself stating it is a "rediscovery of RegNet's already-discovered Pareto-optimal width-progression region" (line 23). A rediscovery is — at top-tier venues — an empirical replication, not a contribution. **MAJOR.**

## B. Empirical rigor

- **PAPER.md:130, FINDINGS.md:11–14 (Phase-8 numbers)** — The three "winners" are evaluated at **n=3 seeds** on CIFAR-100 with a single 30-epoch config. No paired test (sign test, Wilcoxon), no 95% CI, no MAS error analysis. The gate "min-leader-seed > max-baseline-seed" is a **non-standard, low-power ordinal test** equivalent to a one-sided sign test with α≈0.125. The paper does NOT report this α. **BLOCKER.**
- **PAPER.md:130, 134, 138 (lead floors "outside seed noise")** — `pair_gm_pdw` lead floor +0.99 pp, `slot_act_sine` +1.04 pp, `sg_only_phi_budget` +0.25 pp. Phi_budget's lead floor (+0.25 pp) is **inside** the baseline 3-seed std (0.20 pp on CIFAR-100, FINDINGS:283) by less than 1σ on the conservative side. Calling it "outside seed noise" is **statistically unsupported**. **BLOCKER.**
- **PAPER.md:154–156 (§6.1 H41 correction)** — The post-fix β-only number 0.8394 vs baseline 0.8478 (Δ ≈ −1 pp) is reported at **single seed**. No claim of any magnitude should be made at n=1. The original "−33 pp" was also single-seed. The paper is reasoning across single-seed measurements as if they were estimates with known variance. **BLOCKER.**
- **PAPER.md:194 (Limitations)** — Admits "Single-seed for most single-prior sweep rows. Only 3 tags carry 3-seed error bars." This is the burying-the-lede confession: the entire 35-row CIFAR-10 sweep that drives §6.1, §6.2, §6.3, and the 51%-non-PASS audit framing is **n=1**. A single-seed sweep cannot establish a 51% non-PASS rate as a fact; it can only establish 51% non-PASS in this one seed-0 universe. **MAJOR.**
- **PAPER.md:155–156 (H41 reclassification)** — "Reddi 2018's non-convergence proof for β2 < 0.95 is asymptotic and does not yet bite at 12 ep" — but no asymptotic experiment was run. The paper cites a theoretical result to **excuse** the empirical regression rather than test it. This is `audits/PAPER_GAP_G5.md`'s WRONG_TEST tier surfacing in the paper itself. **MAJOR.**
- **PAPER.md:202–214 (§7.3.1 screening vs evaluation)** — This section is the paper's most dangerous move. It **retroactively reclassifies every single-prior negative** (H41, H42, H44, H47, H48, H50, H80) as "screening data, not evaluation," AFTER the negatives were observed. A protocol that decides post-hoc which experiments "count" as evaluations is unfalsifiable. The paper should pre-register which numbers are screening and which are evaluation BEFORE running anything; doing it after the fact is HARKing (Hypothesizing After the Results are Known). **BLOCKER.**
- **PAPER.md:206–211 (hill-climb plan)** — Phase 9 hill-climb is announced as forthcoming but not yet executed. The paper claims the three Phase-8 winners "have cleared all three layers" (line 212). They have not: layer 1 (per-hypothesis hill-climb) is explicitly described as "in progress" (line 208, §7.4 line 224). Submitting a paper that claims clearance of a gate the paper itself describes as not yet run is **misrepresentation**. **BLOCKER.**
- **PAPER.md:154 (H41 eps confound)** — The paper acknowledges the −33 pp was an eps-confound and the β-only number is −1 pp. But the audit also discovered that `golden_momentum` was a 1-step-saturating schedule (FINDINGS:67, PAPER_GAP_G5:75–80) — and yet `pair_gm_pdw` USES golden_momentum as one of its three orthogonal axes. The paper does NOT engage whether the +1.34 pp `pair_gm_pdw` lift is from H48's broken-but-post-Fixer mechanism or simply from "constant β1=0.382 from epoch 1," which is itself an undiscussed confound. **MAJOR.**
- **PAPER.md:138 (slot_act_sine)** — SIREN activation (Sitzmann 2020) is a well-known technique with established CIFAR/ImageNet ablations elsewhere. The paper claims `slot_act_sine` as a Phase-8 winner without engaging whether the +1.32 pp lift is just SIREN's known signal-frequency benefit (which has nothing to do with golden-ratio inspiration). **MAJOR.**
- **PAPER.md:170 (SOTA comparison)** — "ResNet-20 at 164 epochs reaches ~91.25 % CIFAR-10 top-1. Our 12-epoch screening produces ~84.78 %, a 6.5-pp shortfall." The paper compares 12-ep and 164-ep numbers without engaging that the entire H09 lift (+0.78 pp at 12 ep) is **less than 10%** of the headline 6.5-pp deficit-to-SOTA. The "rediscovery of RegNet Pareto region" is at a regime where the chosen baseline is itself 6.5 pp from any defensible SOTA. **MAJOR.**
- **PAPER.md:103 (51 % non-PASS rate)** — The 51% non-PASS rate is presented as a sociological/methodological finding ("audit independence ... gives this number sampling weight"). But the audit teams were authored within hours by Opus 4.7 agents trained on the same prior literature; their independence is methodological theatre. A 51% non-PASS rate found by 8 sibling agents is NOT calibrated against a baseline (e.g., what % non-PASS would the same audit team find in published NeurIPS papers? in scikit-learn? in PyTorch examples?). Without that baseline the 51% is just a number. **MAJOR.**
- **PAPER.md:122 (H55 zero-bias)** — `bias = (coords @ coords.T).mean(dim=-1) = 0` for vertex-transitive Platonic solids. This is correct math, but the paper presents it as a stunning finding without noting that the implementer agent that wrote the broken code AND the audit agent that caught it AND the Fixer that patched it are all the SAME model class. The "audit caught a bug" narrative deserves engagement of this prompt-engineering caveat. **MINOR.**
- **PAPER.md:140–151 (cumulative defect tally)** — Mixes implementation defects (BROKEN/MAJOR/MINOR) with sci-critic verdicts (NUMEROLOGY/UNFALSIFIABLE) into one table without explaining how these are different dimensions. Treating "code has a bug" and "the idea is numerology" as additive defects is category confusion. **MINOR.**

## C. Reproducibility

- **PAPER.md (entire) — no hyperparameter table** — The paper never lists per-hypothesis hyperparameters (LR, base_wd, batch, optimizer, scheduler, augmentation, label-smoothing eps). It refers to `configs/cifar10_quick.yaml` and `configs/cifar10_sota_smoke.yaml` in §8 of CLAUDE.md but not in the paper itself. A reader who clones the repo at a different commit cannot reproduce. **BLOCKER.**
- **PAPER.md:170 (compute)** — Does NOT mention the 1× RTX 4090 Laptop hardware contract. CLAUDE.md §2 specifies bf16 AMP, batch=256, num_workers=0, KMP_DUPLICATE_LIB_OK=TRUE, OMP_NUM_THREADS=2 (Rules 26). None of this is in the paper. **MAJOR.**
- **PAPER.md:241 (references)** — Most references are abridged with `arXiv:XXXX.XXXXX` only; some lack year/venue/title (e.g., Pittorino 2022 cited by H22 doc is NOT in PAPER.md references). Rule-4-format compliance is incomplete. **MAJOR.**
- **PAPER.md:130, 134 (winning tag identifiers)** — `pair_gm_pdw`, `slot_act_sine`, `sg_only_phi_budget` are mentioned by sweep-tag identifier but the paper never defines the sweep-tag → config-row mapping. A reader cannot trace `pair_gm_pdw` to its concrete `(phi_budget=True, momentum_schedule="golden", phi_decay_wd=True, phi_decay_base=5e-4)` definition without reading `scripts/run_sweep.py`. **MAJOR.**
- **PAPER.md:128 (post-fix realised ratio)** — "Post-fix realized ratio is 1:1.623:2.629 (0.43% max error). The architecture itself changed (widths [40, 48, 64] → [37, 48, 61])." Provides the widths, but does NOT provide the integer-search hyperparameters (param-budget target, ratio tolerance, search grid bounds) needed to re-derive [37, 48, 61] from scratch. **MAJOR.**
- **PAPER.md:152, 158 (composite formula)** — References the SHA-256 fingerprint in §3 of CLAUDE.md but never STATES the composite formula `top1 − 0.05·log10(params_M) − 0.05·log10(latency_ms)` in the paper itself. A reader must follow a link. **MAJOR.**
- **PAPER.md (entire) — no random-seed protocol description** — Says "seed-median composite over `--seeds 0 1 2`" once in §6.1 by reference; never specifies whether seeds are torch+numpy+python, whether cudnn.deterministic=True, whether dataloader workers are seeded, etc. CLAUDE.md Rule 6 says `cudnn.benchmark=True` (the non-deterministic option) is "intentional" — this means runs are NOT bit-reproducible on the same machine. The paper does not engage this. **MAJOR.**

## D. Scope / generalization

- **PAPER.md:195 (no transformer experiments)** — Half the implemented hypotheses (H03, H15, H16, H27, H32, H34, H36, H37, H55, H71) target transformer/LM backbones; NONE were tested. The paper's empirical evidence is CNN-only, 35 rows on CIFAR-10, 5 rows on CIFAR-100. Then §7.4 (line 221) promises ViT-Tiny and decoder-only LM scaffolds as future work. A paper claiming an 84-hypothesis design space cannot ship with half the hypotheses **architecturally untestable in the shipped pipeline**. **BLOCKER.**
- **PAPER.md:184 (H71 IcosaRoPE3D)** — The sole NOVEL+TESTABLE hypothesis is a transformer hypothesis. The paper cannot test it. The paper claims H71 as the highest-rated idea AND admits it has no empirical evidence. This is precisely the kind of "we have a brilliant idea, trust us" framing that NeurIPS reviewers reject. **MAJOR.**
- **PAPER.md:196 (12/30-epoch budget)** — Acknowledges "12/30-epoch budget is the screening, not the convergence regime. Stronger or different priors may emerge at the 164-epoch full SOTA recipe." Then claims `pair_gm_pdw` is a Phase-8 WINNER (line 134) at 30-ep CIFAR-100. The paper cannot simultaneously claim "we are not in the convergence regime" and "we have a definitive winner." **MAJOR.**
- **PAPER.md:197 (CIFAR insufficient testbed)** — Admits "CIFAR-10/CIFAR-100 is not a sufficient testbed for several of the equivariance/topological hypotheses." Then audits those hypotheses on CIFAR anyway and rates them as NUMEROLOGY/FALSIFIED (e.g., H22 toroidal, see `hypotheses/g3_topologies_graphs/H22_toroidal_phi_closure.md` lines 20–25: the falsifier explicitly says "tiled-texture or wrap-aware synthetic dataset," but the project tested on upright CIFAR-10 and recorded a NUMEROLOGY/UNFALSIFIABLE verdict). This is **testing on the wrong dataset and then concluding the hypothesis fails**. **BLOCKER.**
- **PAPER.md:221 (future work — transformer track)** — Promises wiring ViT-Tiny and decoder-only LM scaffolds + WikiText-2 PPL. None of this is in scope of the submission. Per NeurIPS standards, a paper cannot claim contributions whose evaluation is "future work" in the same submission. **MAJOR.**
- **PAPER.md:223 (Tiny ImageNet / MedMNIST / rotated CIFAR / Spherical MNIST)** — All listed as future work. The paper's empirical evidence is therefore confined to one task family (image classification) and one resolution class (32×32). No generalization claims are defensible. **MAJOR.**

## F. Threats to validity (the strongest counterarguments NOT engaged)

- **PAPER.md (entire) — selection bias on the 84 hypotheses** — The 84-hypothesis design space is itself non-random: it is curated by an LLM agent reading a source PDF on nature-inspired networks. The paper's audit operates on a population whose members were selected for plausibility, not coverage. The "1 NOVEL+TESTABLE / 81 critiqued" rate could be an artifact of selection. **BLOCKER.**
- **PAPER.md:202–214 (post-hoc protocol amendment)** — As above (Section B). The strongest counterargument to the paper is "you reclassified every negative as 'screening' after seeing it lose." The paper does not engage this. **BLOCKER.**
- **PAPER.md:132–134 (pair_gm_pdw)** — The strongest counterargument: `pair_gm_pdw` = (phi_budget post-fix) + (golden_momentum constant-β1=0.382 floor) + (phi_decay_wd geometric per-layer wd). Each of these is a regularizer/optimizer with an analog in standard literature (RegNet allocator, low-β1 Adam, layerwise decay LR/wd). The lift could be entirely explained by **any tuning of any three regularization knobs from baseline defaults**. The paper does not run a control where 3 NON-φ regularization axes are stacked. Without that control, the φ-content is decorative. **BLOCKER.**
- **PAPER.md:138 (slot_act_sine)** — Strongest counterargument: SIREN at ω=1 is just `sin(x)`, a smooth bounded activation. Replacing ReLU with `sin(x)` is a known activation-engineering move with no φ content. The "Phase-8 winner" framing buries that this result has zero connection to nature-inspired priors. **BLOCKER.**
- **PAPER.md:128 (post-fix H09)** — Strongest counterargument: the post-fix 0.5741 CIFAR-100 median is 0.6 pp BELOW the pre-fix broken-code 0.5805 median. The paper frames this as "the audit correctly caught a fortuitously-high pre-fix seed-0 result" but an equally consistent explanation is that the corrected widths [37, 48, 61] are simply slightly worse than the broken widths [40, 48, 64]. The paper does not test whether the pre-fix widths at the broken ratio outperform the post-fix widths at the correct ratio at iso-cost. **MAJOR.**
- **PAPER.md (entire) — multiple-comparisons correction missing** — The 35-row CIFAR-10 sweep at α≈0.05 expects ~1.75 false-positive winners by chance. The paper reports exactly one positive (H09) without any Holm-Bonferroni or Benjamini-Hochberg correction. The lift could be a single false discovery in a 35-comparison family. **BLOCKER.**
- **PAPER.md:100–104 (audit defect tally as evidence)** — The 51% non-PASS rate is used to argue the audit "found real defects." But the same Opus 4.7 agent class wrote both the buggy code and the audit. The audit has no false-positive rate measurement (e.g., re-audit known-good third-party code). The high non-PASS rate is **as compatible with hyperactive auditing** as with prevalent bugs. **MAJOR.**
- **PAPER.md (entire) — no comparison to non-φ baseline architecture tweaks** — The paper compares nature-inspired priors against an unmodified ResNet-20. It does NOT compare against (a) tuned ResNet-20 (different LR/wd at iso-compute), (b) RegNetX-200MF (the direct literature analog), or (c) random non-φ width allocations matched in monotone-progression. Without these controls, every "win" could be a tuning artifact of comparing nature-inspired-tuned vs untuned baseline. **BLOCKER.**
- **PAPER.md:24–27 (§1.2 why audit was necessary)** — Claims "every test file in the repo had been written by an implementer agent trained to make tests pass." The strongest counterargument: the FIXER agents that patched the bugs are from the SAME model family that wrote the shape-only tests. The audit's "we caught their bugs" narrative depends on assuming the auditor agents are more reliable than the implementer agents — but the paper provides no calibration of that assumption. **MAJOR.**

## Required revisions before resubmission

1. **PAPER.md:9, 132–138, 212, 228–231** — Resolve the internal contradiction. Pick ONE of: (a) "the protocol is the contribution, all empirical claims are illustrative" — then strip the abstract/§5.5/§5.6 headline framing, OR (b) "we have three Phase-8 winners" — then defend the n=3 statistics with a paired Wilcoxon + Holm-Bonferroni at α=0.01 across the multi-row family and post 95% CIs for every reported pp delta.
2. **PAPER.md:202–214** — Either delete §7.3.1 entirely and accept the negatives as falsifications, OR move it to a "Pre-registration" section dated BEFORE Phase 8 was run (with git commit hash proving pre-registration timing). The current placement is unfalsifiable HARKing.
3. **PAPER.md:130, 134, 138 (Phase-8 numbers)** — Add a formal statistical test for every "outside seed noise" claim: paired Wilcoxon, Holm-Bonferroni across the screen, 95% bootstrap CI on top-1 difference. Drop the "min-leader > max-baseline" ordinal-gate construction or characterize it as a one-sided sign test with α=0.125.
4. **PAPER.md:132–134 (pair_gm_pdw refutation of H50)** — Add a CONTROL row: a 3-axis NON-φ regularizer stack (e.g., uniform-allocation + low-β1=0.4 constant + per-layer-linear-decay-wd at iso-budget) to test whether the +1.34 pp lift is from φ-content or from "any 3 regularization axes tuned."

11. **PAPER.md:241–246 (references)** — Audit every citation for Rule-4 format compliance; add venue/year for Sitzmann SIREN, Pittorino 2022 (cited in H22 but absent from references), and verify Islam 2025 arXiv:2510.03511 publication status.

## Recommended further work

- Run a true cross-domain replication of the audit + Fixer protocol on a second autoresearch repo (tabular or medical) and report whether the same skill-pack discovers a comparable defect distribution. This would substantively support the "content-agnostic protocol" claim.
- Pre-register the Phase-9 hill-climb (knobs, ranges, seed protocol, statistical test, success criteria) BEFORE running it. A pre-registration commit hash in the paper is the cheapest way to neutralize the HARKing concern.
- Run a single H71 IcosaRoPE3D ViT-Tiny smoke on rotated MNIST/CIFAR. Even a negative result on the sole NOVEL+TESTABLE hypothesis converts the contribution from "we have an idea" to "we tested our best idea."
- Add a meta-analysis: of the 30 DERIVATIVE+TESTABLE verdicts, run a stratified sample (5–7) at the literature-canonical recipe (longer training, larger budget) and report whether they reproduce the literature's claimed gains. This converts §5.7 "what survives" from a verdict list into evidence.
- Run an explicit sample-efficiency comparison vs RegNet/timm at iso-compute. This addresses the "rediscovery of RegNet Pareto region" framing head-on.
- Report bootstrap CIs for every pp delta. The composite metric SHA-256 fingerprint is exactly the kind of rigorous infrastructure that deserves matching statistical rigor in the headline numbers.
